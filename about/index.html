Preface

In the steadfast era of futuristic advancements and new innovations popping up every second at an exponential rate, traditional crafts and practices take a backseat. Gradually declining to the nooks and corners of the country, quite a lot of crafts have begun to be detached to the people once they were commonly associated and cherished with.

Through this initiative we aim to understand and amplify the beauty and necessity of the metalwork which is so prominent in moulding historical artefacts and adorning places pan india. From pious associations, with temples imagery to the interiors to the relics of Ancient India, crafting goods with metals goes a long way back, etched deep in shaping the history of India. From commoners to royalty, what was once an abundant sight, isnâ€™t anymore.

Virtually interacting with the artisans of Velachery amidst the pandemic, helped us gain a lot of insight on the practices, processes, historical connotations of the work they do and also shed light on the impact of modernisation on such intricate and devoted crafts of India. Tinkering our way through, we gained a deeper understanding of the craft, its associated communities, their lives, and the dynamics it possesses in the present day.
